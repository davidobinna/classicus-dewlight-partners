Unveiling Our Legal Corps: Meet the Pillars of Our Practice

Meet Daniel Yeri Onyinki. A Distinguished Legal Luminary Pioneering Excellence in Litigation, Corporate Law, and Property Transactions.

Preliminary Statement

Daniel Yeri Onyinki stands as an esteemed legal professional, boasting a distinguished record in litigation, maritime law practice , corporate law, and property transactions. In his role as one of the founding Partners of Classicus & Dewlight Legal Practice LLP, Daniel brings uncommon wealth of experience and expertise to the firm's endeavors. His unwavering commitment to delivering exceptional legal services has propelled him to successfully represent clients in a myriad of high-profile litigious actions. Notable among these are cases involving esteemed entities such as Asset Management Corporation of Nigeria (AMCON), Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC), Nigeria Deposit Insurance Corporation (NDIC), Federal Ministry of Power (FMP), Niger Delta Development Commission (NDDC), Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC), Nigeria Police Force (NPF), as well as numerous state Governments and private corporations. Daniel's exceptional grasp of corporate law and dispute resolution equips him to provide strategic advice and representation in the most complex legal matters. In the realm of property transactions, Daniel showcases a remarkable ability to navigate intricate legal frameworks with precision. His expertise extends to conducting due diligence, perfecting property titles, managing estates, ensuring regulatory compliance, and negotiating and drafting contracts for construction and development projects. Renowned for his meticulous approach and unwavering attention to detail, Daniel has earned a reputation for consistently delivering successful outcomes for his clients. Before partnering to establish Classicus & Dewlight Partners, Daniel served as a Senior Associate at George Ikoli & Okagbue, where he played a pivotal role in handling a diverse array of legal matters; particularly the controversies surrounding the acquisition of OPL 245 by Malabu Oil & Gas, Daniel collaborated with lawyers in; British Virgin Island, England and Italy to advise and provided legal representation in Court to Malabu Oil & Gas and AA Oil & Gas on varieties of legal issues cutting across; British Virgin Island, England and Italy. His experience spanned litigations across multiple states in Nigeria and Europe, encompassing criminal matters, civil matters, fundamental human rights actions, and debt recovery actions involving government institutions. Daniel holds a Bachelor of Laws (LL.B) degree with Second Class Honours (Upper Division) from the prestigious Niger Delta University and is a qualified legal practitioner, having been called to the Nigerian Bar. Committed to remaining at the forefront of legal advancements, he has pursued certifications in various areas, including human rights in the digital age and capital defense training. With an unwavering commitment to excellence, Daniel consistently provides comprehensive legal advice, exceptional client service, and strategic representation. His ability to navigate complex legal challenges and his exemplary advocacy skills have firmly established him as a trusted advocate and legal advisor.
